About TestDNA.bio

TestDNA.bio is a small, independent information site that helps people understand DNA testing before they buy an at-home kit. The goal is simple: cut through marketing language and give you clear, realistic expectations of what a DNA test can and cannot do.

Who is behind the site?

TestDNA.bio is operated by ronarn (Norway). The same owner runs Genectics.com, an independent hub for DNA test reviews and comparisons.

Rather than building one large, cluttered website, the idea is to use focused, descriptive domains like TestDNA.bio and DNAKit.bio for clear, topic-specific pages that send visitors to the main comparison engine on Genectics.com.

What TestDNA.bio does (and does not) do

What we do

  • Explain how DNA testing works in simple language.
  • Outline differences between ancestry, health and mixed DNA tests.
  • Highlight key questions to ask about accuracy and privacy.
  • Link to deeper comparisons and reviews on Genectics.com.

What we don't do

  • Sell DNA kits directly on TestDNA.bio.
  • Offer medical or legal advice.
  • Guarantee that one specific provider is “best” for everyone.
  • Collect detailed personal data about visitors.

Relationship with Genectics.com

Most links on TestDNA.bio point to Genectics.com, which is built as a neutral comparison hub for DNA tests. On Genectics.com you'll find:

  • Side-by-side comparisons of major DNA test providers.
  • Notes on accuracy, privacy policies and region coverage.
  • Where relevant, affiliate links to some providers.

If you choose to buy a kit after clicking a link on Genectics.com, the site may receive an affiliate commission from the provider—at no extra cost to you. This helps keep the content online and up to date.

Why use .bio domains for DNA testing?

The .bio extension is a natural fit for biology, genetics and health topics. Domains like TestDNA.bio, DNAKit.bio and Retinol.bio are designed to be:

  • Memorable and self-explanatory.
  • Easy to understand for non-technical visitors.
  • Good entry points into deeper, more detailed resources.
