DNA Test 101 ‚Äì What a DNA Test Actually Measures

Consumer DNA tests analyze hundreds of thousands of SNPs (genetic markers) to estimate ancestry and identify relatives who share DNA segments. Results depend on reference datasets, statistical models, and how your DNA compares to population samples.

What DNA tests can help with

  • Ancestry / ethnicity estimates (model-based)
  • Relative matching and shared DNA segments
  • Migration patterns and historical region signals
  • Optional traits or health-style insights (provider-dependent)

What DNA tests cannot guarantee

  • Exact percentages or precise locations
  • Definitive medical diagnoses
  • Perfect coverage for underrepresented regions
  • Unchanging results (updates happen over time)

Types of DNA Tests

Most at-home DNA tests fall into three practical categories:

Ancestry tests

Focus on ethnicity estimates and relative matching. Best for genealogy and family discovery.

Health / trait reports

Include selected traits, carrier status or risk markers where permitted. Informational only.

Combined tests

Offer both ancestry and health-style reporting in one kit for a broader overview.

How Accurate Are DNA Tests?

Accuracy depends on reference datasets, statistical models, database size and regional coverage. For well-sampled populations, ancestry estimates can be reasonably consistent; for smaller or under-sampled regions, results may be broader and can change.

Relative matching (finding close relatives) is often the most reliable aspect of DNA testing across providers.

DNA Test Privacy & Data Storage

Before testing, consider how long companies store your DNA sample, whether you can request deletion and sample destruction, and if data may be shared with researchers or third parties.

Policies differ widely. Some providers delete samples after processing; others store them for future updates unless you opt out.

Common Questions About DNA Tests

Can DNA tests identify unknown family members?

Often, yes. Database growth makes it possible to identify close and distant relatives, sometimes indirectly through shared matches.

Can DNA results conflict between companies?

Yes. Reference datasets and algorithms differ, so ethnicity estimates can vary. Close relative matching is typically more consistent.
